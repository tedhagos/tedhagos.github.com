Words of Clever Men

The purpose of design is to create a clean and relatively simple internal structure, sometimes this is called an architecture. A design is the end product of a design process. It involves balancing a set of competing requirements. The product of design are models that allow us to reason about our structures, make tradeoffs when requirement conflicts, and in general provide a blue print for implementation

– Bjarne Stroustrup

A complex system that works is invariably found to have evolved from a simple system that worked. A complex system designed from scratch never works and cannot be patched up to make it to work. You have to start over, beginning from a simple working system

– Booch, Rumbaugh, Jacobson

To truly understand OOP, the concepts abstraction, polymorphism, inheritance and modularity needs to be understood

– Shepard and Wingo, MFC internals

The programming challenge is a large scale exercise of applied abstraction and thus requires the abilities of a formal mathematician and a competent engineer

– Edgar Djikstra

Continuous effort - not strength nor intelligence - is the key to unlocking our potential

– Winston Churchhill

A lonely impulse of delight

– W.B. Yeats

Knowledge must be gained by ourselves. Mankind may supply us with facts; but the results, even if they agree with the previous ones, must be the work of our own mind.

– Benjamin Disraeli
